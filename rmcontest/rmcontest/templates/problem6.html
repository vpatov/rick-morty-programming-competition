In his assigned reading there are a total of 10 documents, each document separated by five dashes "-----". Find the term with the highest TF-IDF (term frequency - inverse document frequency).

TF-IDF is a measure of how significant a term is to a document, given a collection of documents. A simplified version of it can be calculated as such: 


	tf(term) = (# occurences of term in document / total # of terms in document )
	idf(term) = log(# documents that include term / # documents)
	### log is the natural log. Python's math module has this function.

	tf-idf(term) = tf(term) * idf(term)

Note that the TF of a term is specific to the document - the same term can have different TFs for different documents. The IDF is global per corpus (a corpus is a collection of documents). The TF-IDF of a term in a document is the product of the TF and IDF, and it is specific to a document.

What is the term with the highest TF-IDF?
